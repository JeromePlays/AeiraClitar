Boracay: A Complete Travel Guide to the Island Paradise

Boracay, a small island and one of the top islands in the Philippines, is a tropical paradise known for its powdery white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, sunsets, and vibrant nightlife. Recognized as one of the world's best islands, Boracay offers an unforgettable experience for every traveler. Here's a complete guide to help you plan your trip to this stunning destination.

Tourist Destination Information

Boracay is located in the Western Visayas region of the Philippines, under the municipality of Malay, Aklan. The island's most famous attraction is White Beach, a 4-kilometer of pristine sand lined with resorts, restaurants, and bars. Other notable spots include.

Puka Shell Beach

Located on the northern tip of the island, Puka Beach offers a more rustic experience with natural beauty and an untouched feel. Known for its coral and puka shells scattered along the shore, it’s perfect for those seeking a quieter escape and a more rugged beach experience.

Bulabog Beach

Located on the island’s eastern side, Bulabog Beach is a hub for water sports enthusiasts, especially for kitesurfing and windsurfing. It has a vibrant atmosphere with a backdrop of colorful sails and a laid-back vibe in the evening.

Read More
Mount Luho

Mt. Luho is the tallest peak on Boracay Island, standing at about 330 feet above sea level. Located in Aklan, its viewing deck offers panoramic views of the province and the white sand shores lining it.

Read More
Willy's Rock

Willy's Rock is a volcanic rock formation that is located along the shallow waters of Station 1 in Boracay. Apart from its unique shape, it has also become a popular attraction due to the shrine dedicated to the Blessed Virgin Mary that sits on its surface.

How to Get There?

All tourists heading to Boracay must travel through Caticlan, a barangay on the Aklan mainland. The area has two ports for reaching the island, depending on the season: Caticlan Jetty Port and Tabon Port. There's no need to worry about choosing the right one, as they are close to each other, and local drivers are always aware of which port is in use.

The first step to reach Boracay is to make your way to Caticlan. You have several options.

  • By plane, via Caticlan Airport
  • By plane, via Kalibo Airport
  • By ferry, via 2GO Travel

    • Two airports serve the island: Kalibo International Airport (KLO), located about two hours away, and Caticlan International Airport (MPH), also known as Boracay Airport, which is just a 15-minute drive from either sea port. While all major airlines in the Philippines operate flights to both airports, Caticlan offers more frequent options throughout the day.

    Therefore, if you're traveling from Manila, the quickest and most convenient route to Boracay is via Caticlan Airport.

    You'll also need to pay a terminal fee at Caticlan Jetty Port and an environmental fee to enter Boracay. Local tourists pay 150 pesos for the terminal fee and 150 pesos for the environmental fee, while foreign tourists pay 300 pesos for the environmental fee.

    For adventurous types of people,get your adrenaline pumping with Boracay Island’s wide range of water sports such as:

  • Parasailing
  • Jet Skiing
  • Paddleboarding
  • Diving
    • Island Hopping

    Make the most of your trip by exploring islands and beaches beyond White Beach, come visit nearby islands like.

  • Crystal Cove
  • Crocodile Island
    • Sunset Sailling

    Sunset at the beach is an impeccable thing to see. It is a must to take a paraw (traditional sailboat) for the best view to enjoy the stunning sunset.

    Nightlife

    Drinking on White Beach and beachfront parties are now limited to approved bars. It’s a great chance to explore Boracay Island’s lively bars and enjoy the island’s nightlife. Enjoy vibrant bars and clubs like Epic, Exit Bar, and Paraw Club.
